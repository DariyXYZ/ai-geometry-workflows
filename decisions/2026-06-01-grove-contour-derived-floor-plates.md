# 2026-06-01 - Grove contour-derived floor plates

## Decision

For Grove at Grand Bay and similar rotating rectangular towers, intermediate
floor plates must be generated by Rhino `Contour` from a temporary loft between
authoritative control sections, then used directly as final floor outlines.

## Why

Manual interpolation between rotated rectangles produced non-orthogonal,
warped floors. The architectural source describes orthogonal floor plates that
change rotation through the lower tower zone. Rhino `Contour` preserves the
horizontal floor-plane intersections and avoids arbitrary corner
correspondence.

## Rule

- Use source sections: floor 1, floor 14, roof or other known datums.
- Build a temporary loft only to derive sections.
- Run `Contour` at floor elevations.
- Hide the loft mass.
- Build floor slabs directly from the resulting contour curves.
- Keep reference underlays and helper plates below floor 1.

## Final Correction

The contour-derived curve is the exterior slab or balcony edge, not the glazing
line. For balcony towers, final geometry must split the facade system into:

- thick floor slab / balcony plate from the contour;
- inward-offset glass plane;
- exterior rail or edge profile;
- roof/mechanical volumes on explicit height datums.

Placing glass flush with the contour hides the balcony depth and should be
treated as a failed model even when the twist and floor outlines are correct.
